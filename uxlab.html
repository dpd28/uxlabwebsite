The User Experience Lab

The User Experience (UX) Lab at the University of Miami is an academic-based research facility that supports interdisciplinary research on how people use information systems. Our main function is to inform web, mobile, gaming, and software interface design based on human attitudes, emotions, and performance data. We use a variety of research methods to collect and analyze findings, including focus groups, ethnography, participatory design, expert reviews, usability testing, facial expression analysis, and eye tracking.

The UX Lab was also designed to support the University of Miami’s M.F.A. in Interactive Media and provides educational opportunities to students who are focused on obtaining careers in User Experience and Interaction Design. We also offer a full range of UX research services to internal clients.

The UX Lab is an academic-based research facility that provides the following services:

  • Usability Testing
  • A/B Testing
  • Expert Review
  • Visual Attention and Emotional Response Studies
  • Design Research (Interviews, Focus Groups, Survey, Card Sorts)
  • HCI Research

The UX Lab features:

  • Eye tracking data using a Tobii X60 eye tracker and Tobii Studio software
  • Event logging
  • Professional audio/video control and recording equipment
  • Mobile observation equipment (camera and platform)
  • Observation room with one-way mirror and multiple monitors to display participants’ screen, live eye gaze views, and other video feeds to facilitate observation

Our team of experienced UX researchers is passionate about creating great user experiences. Please contact Dr. Millet for more information at bmillet@miami.edu.

The User Experience Lab gathers evidence on user behavior to design better interactions between people and technologies.
